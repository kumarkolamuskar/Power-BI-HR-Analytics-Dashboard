# Power BI HR Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition and workforce trends using the IBM HR Analytics dataset. The dashboard provides insights into employee demographics, satisfaction, performance, and factors influencing attrition.

## Tools & Technologies Used
- Power BI Desktop
- Power Query
- DAX Measures
- Calculated Columns
- Data Modeling
- Interactive Dashboard Design

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Executive Overview
Provides high-level HR metrics:
- Total Employees: 1,470
- Active Employees: 1,233
- Attrition Count: 237
- Attrition Rate: 16.12%
- Average Age: 36.92 years
- Average Monthly Income: 6,502.93

### 2. Attrition Analysis
Analyzes attrition patterns by:
- Age Group
- Marital Status
- Income Group
- Business Travel
- Job Role

### 3. Employee Performance & Satisfaction
Examines factors affecting employee retention:
- Environment Satisfaction
- Work-Life Balance
- Job Satisfaction
- Performance Rating
- Years Since Last Promotion

## Key Insights
- Employees aged 26–35 experienced the highest attrition.
- Research & Development had the highest employee turnover.
- Male employees accounted for a larger share of attrition.
- Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives showed high turnover.
- Employees with lower job satisfaction and work-life balance were more likely to leave.
- Lower-income employees experienced higher attrition.

## Business Recommendations
- Improve retention strategies for employees aged 26–35.
- Review compensation for lower-income groups.
- Enhance promotion and career growth opportunities.
- Focus on employee engagement in high-risk departments.
- Strengthen work-life balance initiatives.
